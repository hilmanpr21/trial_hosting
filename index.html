Employee Well-being and Facilities Services

Well-being has become everyone's main concern in recent years, especially when it comes to work environments. Offices with happier employees showed higher productivity and better financial performance (Seligman and Adler, 2019). On the other hand, well-being shows negative relation with hour work which mean the longer working hour the lower employee’s well-being (Diener, Kahneman and Helliwell, 2010). Thus, to boost employee productivity, companies tried some strategies to maintain employee’s well-being. One of them is by providing facilities management services in the office which cover employee’s daily needs such as health, food, and stationary supplies (Anker Jensen et al., 2012; Arampatzi and Burger, 2020). Not only soft services but hard services that include physical infrastructure also being provided(Anker Jensen et al., 2012). Thus, the increase of labour and the development of built environment happen together to fulfil worker needs. Hence, urban development happen significantly around workplaces.

However, since pandemic covid-19, people are have been forced to work from home. Meanwhile they still need to access service facilities to support their daily productivity. Therefore, this website not only tell story of urban development through workplace perspective but also assess wherever we work now, does this area has all the service facilities needed.
